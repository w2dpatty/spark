      Spark

      A rough idea becomes a plan you can send.

      What Spark does

      Describe a project in a sentence and Spark turns it into everything you need to plan it and pitch it:

      • Structures the work — a name, brief, task list with time estimates, the open questions, and the real risks.
      • Prices it — your estimates × your rate = a live quote with a best–worst range.
      • Maps how it connects — a brain map that shows dependencies, the critical path, and bottlenecks.
      • Hands you a client-ready proposal — a polished PDF (three looks, three formats) you can send today.

      …and you can hold the pen at any point — start from a blank canvas, have Spark organize your own draft, or let it write the first version.

      The best parts

      • A map that thinks — not just boxes. Draw dependencies and it finds the critical path, the bottleneck, what can run in parallel, and answers graph questions with AI.
      • You stay in control — human, AI, or any mix. Spark assists; it never hijacks your plan.
      • It learns your estimating — notices when you run longer or shorter than its guesses and calibrates future plans to you.
      • Spark lenses — instant angles on any project: Pitch, Budget, Lean MVP, Pre-mortem.
      • Private by design — everything stays in your browser; you bring your own AI key.

      Coming next planned

      • Accounts & hosted keys — sign up and it just works, no key to paste.
      • Real-time coworking — plan together with your team: presence, comments, live edits.
      • Post-project learning — a quick debrief (planned vs. actual) that sharpens your estimates and risk-spotting over time.
      • More handoff — editable Word/DOCX export, and pushing tasks to Notion / Trello / Linear.

      Directions, not promises — shaped by what early users like you tell us.
